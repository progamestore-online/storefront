How subscriptions work

You subscribe to a game (not the platform). One @progamestore account holds all your subscriptions; each one bills separately. There is no platform-wide "Pro plan" — you pay only for the games you actually play.

Payments go through Stripe. The platform takes a 10% commission; the rest goes to the developer who built the game. Refunds are processed by the platform: prorated within the first 30 days, no refunds after.

10% platform commission — here's where it goes. Cloudflare Durable Object charges (the WebSocket multiplayer infrastructure) are the bulk of the platform cost. The rest covers Stripe fees, the auth + leaderboard workers, and the team that runs compliance + uptime monitoring. Developers keep 90% of what their game earns.

What you get with a subscription

  • Unlimited play time — no per-game caps. Subscription pays for the underlying multiplayer infrastructure.
  • Cloud saves — your game state syncs across phone, tablet, laptop.
  • Multiplayer matchmaking — subscribed players are matched together by default.
  • Server-side AI — some games (chess) include a Stockfish engine running server-side. Token-billed AI features (e.g. LLM opponents) might be a small per-game add-on.
  • No ads, no upsells, no tracking — same brand-consistent shell as FreeGameStore. The subscription is the entire business model.

Trial & cancellation

  • 7-day free trial on every game. Card required to start the trial; no charge until day 7. Cancel during the trial = no charge.
  • Cancel any time from your account page. You keep access until the end of the current billing period.
  • 30-day prorated refund — if you cancel within the first 30 days of a subscription, we'll refund the unused portion. After 30 days, no refunds (cancel still works).

How developers set prices

Game developers pick their price when they publish, with a minimum of $1/month and a maximum of $20/month. Prices can change between subscription periods (never mid-period for existing subscribers). The platform doesn't dictate pricing — we trust developers to charge what their game's worth.

Compared to FreeGameStore

FreeGameStore is genuinely free forever. The two stores share the same brand, the same SDK, the same compliance bar. The difference is what each can offer:

  • FreeGameStore games are single-player only. State lives on your device. Free because the platform doesn't pay for server-side anything.
  • ProGameStore games are server-authoritative. Real-time multiplayer, cloud sync, persistent worlds. Subscription pays the per-GB-second Durable Object bill.

If you only want single-player, stay on FreeGameStore. If you want to play chess against a friend in real time over a WebSocket connection, that's why this side exists.
